## 4. WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Administering Agency** | U.S. DOL / ETA, administered locally through Workforce Development Boards |
| **Official URL** | https://www.apprenticeship.gov/investments-tax-credits-and-tuition-support/workforce-innovation-and-opportunity-act |
| **Federal Resources Playbook** | https://www.apprenticeship.gov/sites/default/files/playbook.pdf |
| **Find Your Local Board** | https://www.careeronestop.org/ |

### Three Ways Shops Access WIOA Funding:

**A. Related Training & Instruction (RTI)**
- Funded through Individual Training Accounts (ITAs)
- Your program must be on the state's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)
- Registered Apprenticeship programs are often automatically eligible for ETPL
- Can fund classroom/technical instruction for apprentices

**B. On-the-Job Training (OJT) Contracts**
- OJT contracts typically reimburse **50% of the apprentice's wage rate**
- Funded through contracts (not ITAs)
- Can cover one or multiple apprentices

**C. Supportive Services**
- WIOA formula funds can provide transportation, childcare, tools, books, uniforms
- Can be bundled with ITA funding

| üí∞ **Potential Amount** | Varies by local board ‚Äî OJT reimbursement is typically 50% of wages; ITAs vary by state ($2,000‚Äì$10,000+) |
| üìä **Difficulty** | Moderate (requires relationship with local Workforce Development Board) |
| üè∑Ô∏è **Categories** | Training, Wages (partial reimbursement), Tools, Books, Supportive Services |
| ‚è∞ **Deadline Type** | Rolling ‚Äî funds allocated annually to local boards |
| ‚úÖ **Confidence** | **HIGH** ‚Äî apprenticeship.gov source |
| **Requires RA Sponsor Status** | ‚úÖ Yes (for apprenticeship-specific funding) |

> **üí° HOW TO START:** Walk into your local American Job Center (find at careeronestop.org) and say "I'm a registered apprenticeship sponsor and I want to discuss WIOA funding for my apprentices." That's it.

## 5. DoD SkillBridge Program

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Administering Agency** | U.S. Department of Defense |
| **Official URL** | https://skillbridge.osd.mil/ |
| **Industry Partner Page** | https://skillbridge.osd.mil/industry-employers.htm |
| **FAQ** | https://skillbridge.osd.mil/faq.htm |

### What It Is
Service members in their **last 180 days of active duty** can train full-time at your shop. DOD continues paying their salary and benefits. You pay nothing.

### What the Shop Gets
- **Free labor** from a highly motivated, trained service member for up to 6 months
- Pipeline to hire that person after separation
- No wages required (DOD pays them)
- Access to military talent pool

### How to Become a SkillBridge Partner
1. Complete the **Employer Ethics Course** (required)
2. Have clearly defined job training programs
3. Demonstrate high probability of a job for the participant afterward
4. Show your industry is in demand with advancement opportunities
5. Apply through skillbridge.osd.mil

### Important Rules
- Service members **cannot receive wages** from the employer during SkillBridge
- Maximum 180 days
- Must be during member's final 180 days on active duty

| üí∞ **Potential Amount** | Value of 6 months of a trained worker at $0 cost (estimated $25,000‚Äì$50,000+ in labor value) |
| üìä **Difficulty** | Moderate (application process, must meet requirements) |
| üè∑Ô∏è **Categories** | Training (you provide), Wages (DOD pays member) |
| ‚è∞ **Deadline Type** | Rolling ‚Äî apply anytime |
| ‚úÖ **Confidence** | **HIGH** ‚Äî skillbridge.osd.mil source |
| **Requires RA Sponsor Status** | ‚ùå No (but having one strengthens your application) |

## 7. Workforce Pell Grants (NEW ‚Äî Implementation Underway)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Administering Agency** | U.S. Department of Education |
| **Key Source** | https://www.newamerica.org/education-policy/edcentral/can-workforce-pell-support-apprenticeship-yes-but/ |
| **ED Guidance** | https://www.ed.gov/media/document/2025-ahead-what-types-of-programs-could-be-eligible-workforce-pell-grants-112701.pdf |
| **JFF Analysis** | https://www.jff.org/blog/budget-bill-expands-pell-eligibility-whats-next-for-students-and-providers/ |

### What's Happening
Congress passed **Workforce Pell** in summer 2025, expanding Pell Grant eligibility to shorter-term training programs. This includes the **Related Technical Instruction (RTI)** component of Registered Apprenticeship programs.

### Current Status (as of Feb 2026)
- Draft rules negotiated December 2025
- Proposed regulations expected early 2026 for public comment
- Final rules expected late spring 2026
- **NOT YET AVAILABLE FOR USE** ‚Äî still in rulemaking

### Requirements for RTI to Qualify
- RTI must be delivered by a provider eligible for federal student aid (typically a community college or accredited institution)
- Must meet criteria in the law and forthcoming regulations
- Governors will play a large role in deciding which programs qualify (varies by state)

### Maximum Pell Grant Amount
- Up to **$7,395/year** (2025-26 award year) for eligible students
- Workforce Pell amounts for short-term programs will be prorated

| üí∞ **Potential Amount** | Up to $7,395/year per apprentice (prorated for program length) |
| üìä **Difficulty** | TBD ‚Äî regulations still being written |
| üè∑Ô∏è **Categories** | Tuition (for Related Technical Instruction only) |
| ‚è∞ **Deadline Type** | Rolling (once implemented ‚Äî likely 2026-2027 academic year at earliest) |
| ‚úÖ **Confidence** | **MEDIUM** ‚Äî law passed, but rules not finalized. Implementation details could change. |
| **Requires RA Sponsor Status** | ‚úÖ Yes |

> **‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT:** This is real legislation that passed, but it's NOT available yet. Don't tell shop owners they can get Pell Grants for apprentices today. Coming soon ‚Äî likely late 2026 or early 2027 for first disbursements.

## 8. WOTC (Work Opportunity Tax Credit)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Administering Agency** | IRS (jointly with DOL) |
| **Official URL** | https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/work-opportunity-tax-credit |
| **IRS Notice** | https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/the-work-opportunity-tax-credit-is-available-until-the-end-of-2025 |

### Credit Amounts
| Target Group | Max First-Year Wages Counted | Max Credit (40% rate) |
|-------------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| Most target groups | $6,000 | **$2,400** |
| Qualified veterans (various) | $6,000‚Äì$24,000 | **$2,400‚Äì$9,600** |
| Long-term TANF recipients (2nd year) | $10,000 | **$4,000** |

### Qualifying Target Groups (most relevant to avionics shops)
- **Veterans** (including disabled veterans ‚Äî highest credit)
- Formerly incarcerated individuals
- SNAP recipients
- Long-term unemployed (27+ weeks)
- Designated community residents
- Vocational rehabilitation referrals
- Summer youth employees
- SSI recipients
- Long-term TANF recipients

### How to Claim
1. Complete **IRS Form 8850** within 28 days of the new hire's start date
2. Submit to your state's Designated Local Agency (DLA) / State Workforce Agency
3. Receive certification
4. Claim credit on your tax return using Form 5884

| üí∞ **Potential Amount** | $2,400‚Äì$9,600 per qualifying hire |
| üìä **Difficulty** | Easy (just file Form 8850 within 28 days of hire) |
| üè∑Ô∏è **Categories** | Tax credit (offsets wage costs) |
| ‚è∞ **Deadline Type** | Per-hire (28-day window from start date) |
| ‚úÖ **Confidence** | **HIGH** ‚Äî irs.gov source |
| **Requires RA Sponsor Status** | ‚ùå No |

> **‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL STATUS NOTE:** WOTC is **authorized through December 31, 2025 ONLY**. As of February 2026, **Congress has NOT yet reauthorized it** for 2026 and beyond. Historically, Congress has always reauthorized and retroactively applied it, but there is no guarantee. **Continue filing Form 8850 for new hires** ‚Äî if/when Congress reauthorizes, it will likely be applied retroactively.

**Source:** https://www.experian.com/blogs/employer-services/wotc-renewal-what-employers-should-know-ahead-2025-expiration/

# PART IV: ACTION CHECKLIST FOR SHOP OWNERS

### Step 1: Get Registered (THE PREREQUISITE)
- [ ] Register as a DOL Registered Apprenticeship sponsor
- [ ] **Fastport** (fastport.com) can help with this process, especially for veteran hiring
- [ ] Contact your **Regional Office of Apprenticeship**: https://www.apprenticeship.gov/about-us/regional-offices

### Step 2: Get VA-Approved (FOR VETERAN HIRING)
- [ ] Contact your **State Approving Agency (SAA)**
- [ ] Submit VA Form 22-8865
- [ ] Get your program listed in the GI Bill Comparison Tool

### Step 3: Connect with Local Workforce Board (FOR WIOA FUNDING)
- [ ] Find your board at https://www.careeronestop.org/
- [ ] Get your program on the state's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)
- [ ] Discuss OJT contracts and ITA funding

### Step 4: Apply for State Credits (EASY MONEY)
- [ ] Check your state's apprenticeship tax credit (see table above)
- [ ] File appropriate state tax forms

### Step 5: File WOTC for Every Qualifying Hire
- [ ] File **IRS Form 8850** within 28 days of every new hire
- [ ] Screen all new hires for WOTC qualifying groups

### Step 6: Consider SkillBridge (FREE TALENT PIPELINE)
- [ ] Apply to become a SkillBridge partner at skillbridge.osd.mil
- [ ] Market to transitioning military near your location
