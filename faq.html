What is the AEA/Fastport Apprenticeship Program?

A nationally registered apprenticeship program, approved by the U.S. Department of Labor, for Avionics Technicians (O*NET 49-2091.00, RAPIDS code 0003CB).

AEA partnered with Fastport to create a national program so individual shops don't have to go through the lengthy process of registering their own. Any AEA member shop can join by signing a one-page Employer Acceptance Agreement.

Ref: 29 CFR Part 29 ‚Äî National Apprenticeship Standards

Who is Fastport?

Fastport is a DOL-recognized Standards Recognition Entity (SRE) and intermediary that handles all compliance, paperwork, EEO reporting, and DOL communication for the program.

They serve as the liaison between your shop and the federal government. You focus on training ‚Äî they handle the bureaucracy.

What is AEA's role?

AEA is the Related Technical Instruction (RTI) provider. We deliver 586 hours of structured training across 4 phases through the CAET (Certified Aircraft Electronics Technician) curriculum.

AEA also advocates for the program, connects shops with resources, and supports member adoption.

How is this different from just hiring and training someone?

You're probably already training people informally. A registered apprenticeship formalizes what you're already doing, which unlocks federal and state funding ‚Äî tax credits, wage reimbursements, veteran benefits.

It also gives your apprentice a nationally recognized DOL credential upon completion.

Same work, more money, better retention.

Is DOL going to show up at my shop?

No. Fastport handles all DOL compliance and reporting. DOL may request records, but Fastport manages that communication.

Your obligation is to provide quality training and maintain progress records ‚Äî which the tracking system handles for you.

Ref: 29 CFR ¬ß29.7 ‚Äî Apprenticeship program compliance

‚úÖ How to Join
How do I sign up?

Sign the one-page Employer Acceptance Agreement. That's it.

Fastport registers your shop as a participating employer under the national program standards. No separate DOL application needed.

What are the requirements?
  • At least one journeyworker (experienced avionics tech) for every apprentice ‚Äî 1:1 ratio
  • You need to be an AEA member shop
  • Agree to follow the Work Process Schedule (43 OJT competencies)
  • Ensure your apprentice completes the RTI
How many apprentices can I have?

As many as you have qualified journeyworkers to supervise. The 1:1 ratio means if you have 5 experienced techs, you can have up to 5 apprentices.

What does it cost me?

The Employer Acceptance Agreement is free. RTI through AEA ‚Äî pricing details available from AEA.

But here's the key: the grants and tax credits available to you as a registered apprenticeship sponsor typically far exceed any program costs.

Use the Funding Finder to see what's available in your state.

Can I hire someone new, or does it have to be a current employee?

Either. You can enroll a current employee or hire someone new specifically as an apprentice.

Veterans can enter through Direct Entry, bypassing standard selection procedures.

Ref: 29 CFR ¬ß29.5(b)(21) ‚Äî Direct Entry provisions

How long does the program take?

Approximately 4 years, competency-based. Faster learners who demonstrate competency can progress more quickly.

There's a 12-week probationary period at the start.

What does the apprentice earn?

Progressive wage schedule increasing as they demonstrate competencies. Shops can customize their own wage schedule. Example:

Phase 1$23/hr
Phase 2$24/hr
Phase 3$25/hr
Phase 4$26/hr
Journeyworker$30/hr
What credential does the apprentice earn?

Upon completion, a DOL Certificate of Completion ‚Äî a nationally recognized credential. Combined with CAET certification from AEA, this gives them a strong professional foundation.

The program also aligns with the path to FAA A&P certification and Inspection Authorization.

Can veterans join?

Absolutely. Veterans qualify for Direct Entry ‚Äî meaning they can bypass standard selection procedures. Military avionics training counts as credit for previous experience.

Plus, veterans unlock additional funding:

  • GI Bill ‚Äî $1,500‚Äì$3,000+/month housing allowance
  • COOL ‚Äî $4,000/year for certifications
  • SkillBridge ‚Äî free labor for up to 180 days (DoD pays their salary)
  • VR&E ‚Äî additional support for disabled veterans

Ref: 38 U.S.C. Chapter 33 (GI Bill); DoD Instruction 1322.29 (SkillBridge)

What if my apprentice quits or I need to let them go?

During the 12-week probationary period, either party can cancel without cause.

After probation, cancellation requires due notice and documented reason. Fastport handles the paperwork.

This does NOT jeopardize your program ‚Äî it's a normal part of the process.

Ref: 29 CFR ¬ß29.7(g) ‚Äî Cancellation and deregistration

What are my ongoing obligations?
  1. Provide on-the-job training following the 43-competency Work Process Schedule
  2. Ensure your apprentice completes RTI through AEA
  3. Rate your apprentice's competency progress (0‚Äì5 scale)
  4. Report leave, vacation, or suspension to Fastport
  5. Report when the apprentice completes the program

The AEA tracking system makes all of this simple.

How do I track my apprentice's progress?

AEA provides a tracking system. OJT competencies are checked off by the supervisor as the apprentice demonstrates them on real shop work.

RTI progress is tracked automatically through the training platform. You can see scores, weak areas, and completion percentage at any time.

Do I have to create a training curriculum?

No. AEA provides all RTI content. The OJT is your normal shop work ‚Äî installations, troubleshooting, inspections ‚Äî mapped to 43 specific competencies.

We give you the checklist; you train them the way you already do.

What paperwork do I need to maintain?

The tracking system handles most of it. You'll need:

  • Signed apprenticeship agreement (ETA-671, Fastport provides)
  • Competency rating records (in the tracker)
  • Attendance/leave records

Fastport handles all DOL reporting.

üí∞ Funding & Payment Mechanics
How does my shop receive funding payments?

Each funding stream has its own process. Registered apprenticeship is a "ticket to the dance" ‚Äî it qualifies you for funding, but each source has different triggers and timelines.

State tax credits: You claim them on your annual state tax return. The credit reduces what you owe (or in some states like Colorado, you get a refund even if you owe zero).

WIOA/workforce board funds: Reimbursement payments after you submit proof of wages paid and training completed. Your local American Job Center administers this.

VA benefits (GI Bill): Paid directly to the veteran apprentice by the VA. $0 hits your books ‚Äî it's supplemental income for them.

Federal tax credits (WOTC): Claimed on your federal income tax return after filing IRS Form 8850 within 28 days of hire.

What triggers the actual funding?

It varies by source:

State tax credits: Trigger automatically once you have a registered apprentice. You claim it when you file taxes. Fastport provides the documentation you need.

WIOA funds: Require coordination with your local American Job Center. You sign an OJT agreement with them before hiring. They reimburse based on milestones (usually monthly or quarterly).

VA benefits: Flow directly to the veteran once your shop is VA-approved and the veteran applies. Fastport provides technical assistance navigating VA approval.

Fastport provides technical assistance and coaching throughout all of this. You're not navigating it alone.

What does Fastport do to help with VA approval?

Fastport's subject matter experts provide technical assistance around becoming VA-approved, connecting you with State Approving Agencies (SAA), and navigating the approval process.

This is critical if you want to hire veterans who will use GI Bill benefits. The SAA reviews your training program, ensures it meets VA standards, and issues approval.

Once approved, any veteran you hire can apply for GI Bill housing allowance ($1,500‚Äì$3,000+/month) while working for you.

Do I need to work with my local workforce board?

Yes, for local WIOA/workforce development funds.

Your local American Job Center (part of the workforce board system) administers WIOA On-the-Job Training (OJT) reimbursement ‚Äî which can reimburse up to 50% of wages during training.

Fastport provides assistance and coaching to help you connect with and navigate your local workforce board requirements. Each board operates slightly differently, so local guidance matters.

What internal commitments are required for state programs?

This varies by state. The National Program Standard provides some reciprocity, but each state and local workforce board may have their own requirements:

  • Some states require annual reporting (Fastport handles this)
  • Some workforce boards require pre-approval before hiring (sign OJT agreement first)
  • Some states have specific documentation requirements for tax credit claims (Fastport provides templates)

Fastport provides guides during onboarding training and continuous technical assistance specific to your state and local requirements.

What paperwork is needed when I approve a new apprentice?

You'll need the ETA-671 (Apprentice Agreement) and Voluntary Disability Disclosure Forms.

Successful employers incorporate these into their existing onboarding process ‚Äî same packet as W-4, I-9, and direct deposit forms.

The forms are individualized (pre-filled with your shop info and apprentice info) and available after Fastport's onboarding training, which covers exactly how to complete and submit them.

Who is responsible for submitting apprentice paperwork?

You (the employer):

  • Hire the apprentice
  • Verify previous training/experience (for credit toward competencies)
  • Support RTI completion (ensure apprentice attends training)
  • Provide qualified mentors and OJT supervision
  • Assess competencies and provide ratings
  • Advise Fastport of status changes: cancelled, suspended, or completed

Fastport:

  • Handles all DOL submissions
  • Manages compliance reporting
  • EEO data collection and reporting
  • DOL registration and deregistration
What records do I need to keep?

Employers retain all apprentice documents including:

  • Hiring records (application, interview notes, selection justification)
  • Signed apprenticeship agreement (ETA-671)
  • Related training documentation (RTI attendance, test scores, completion certificates)
  • OJT records (competency evaluations, supervisor sign-offs, progress tracking)
  • Wage information (pay stubs, wage progression documentation)
  • Competency assessments (ratings, evaluations, milestone completions)

The AEA tracking system maintains most of this digitally. You just need to retain copies per your normal business record retention policy (typically 3‚Äì7 years depending on state).

What funding should I pursue as an employer?

Pursue ALL available funding. Don't leave money on the table. Here's the checklist:

  1. WOTC (Work Opportunity Tax Credit) ‚Äî Federal tax credit for hiring from target groups (veterans, SNAP recipients, long-term unemployed). File IRS Form 8850 within 28 days of hire.
  2. WIOA funds ‚Äî Contact your local American Job Center and set up an OJT agreement BEFORE you hire. They reimburse up to 50% of wages.
  3. State tax credits ‚Äî Automatic in most states once you have a registered apprentice. Claim on annual state tax return.
  4. VA Benefits (if hiring veteran) ‚Äî Work with Fastport to get VA-approved. Veteran applies for GI Bill. They get $1,500‚Äì$3,000+/month.
  5. Veteran-specific tax credits ‚Äî Some states offer additional credits for hiring veterans.
  6. Recently announced incentive funding ‚Äî DOL Pay-for-Performance, ABA grants, state-specific programs. Fastport keeps you updated.
